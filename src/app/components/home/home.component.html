Substance Abuse and Mental Health

Is America's Opioid Crisis
the symptom of overarching mental health issues?

As if the pandemic wasn't enough, it's no secret that America finds itself another epidemic: the opium epidemic. As of 2020, the US Department of Health and Human Services reported over 70,000 dead by drug overdose while the CDC declares an economic cost of $78.5 billion per year.

In light of this, Rocky Mountain Poison and Drug Safety Center have been gathering hundreds of thousands of electronic survey responses as part of an effort to gain a better understanding of who might be most vulnerable to, and if we're lucky, maybe even a little bit of insight into why.

Exploring the relationship between mental health disorders and drug abuse










Comparing four mental health disorders across the states

After performing that exploratory analysis just to confirm that in general people diagnosed with mental health disorders were indeed disproportionately negatively affected, it was time to start creating some more sophisticated plots. Thanks to some guidance from the mentors, we realized it would be a very worthwhile pursuit to perform similar calculations and visualizations across all the different states. This would allows us to glean interesting information by comparing different subsets of the data - not only to see how people diagnosed living in different areas might be impacted by this substance abuse phenomenon differently, but also possibly even why. In particular, how might say the numbers may differ in rural states versus urban ones, or possibly if the fact many states were still in the process of legalization of recreational marijuana at the time.
